Shadow Advisory · ~400M€ Exit · 2 Years

I wasn't in the room.
I was behind the person
in the room.

A founder-led tech company. A €400M merger to PE. Two years, three non-delegable phases — and a kind of advisory that most firms don't offer, because most firms don't understand what founders actually need.

Two years · Three phases · Key decisions mapped
Phase I · Year 1
The CEO who had to go — but couldn't
The founder had installed an external CEO to lead toward exit. The fit was wrong. The decision to remove him was overdue — but emotionally charged, legally complex, organisationally treacherous. My role: not HR advisor. The space where the founder could finally ask what he actually wanted — and what he was prepared to do.
Critical decision point
Phase II · Year 1–2
The founder who had to stop running the company
Internal promotion to new CEO — high symbolic value inside the organisation. The founder's instinct: re-intervene. An unstabilised founder in the background will destabilise any leadership team, no matter how well it's configured. This phase was about building a genuinely new identity: non-operating owner.
Hardest phase psychologically
Phase III · Year 2
Shadow advisory in the exit process
As the sale process accelerated: negotiation strategy and personal load-bearing in the same sessions. How the combined management team was structured wasn't an HR question — it was a valuation question. That work happened here, not in the boardroom.
~400M€ transaction closes
